My Portfolio

One of my first projects was working on the operating for Apples' latest project: the Apple Ki11 Autonomous Attack Drone aka "The Killbot".

Steve Jobs himself asked me to work on the operating system for this project. He told me himself that with these killbots, he would finally be able to exact his revenge on that "self-rightous nerd". Sadly, he passed away before the code was finished.

Oh, and you know the Killbots automated kill-counter shutoff? That was MY idea.

After finishing work on the KillBot project, I did some freelance work for award-winning physicist Dr. Seth Brundle.

Dr. Brundle was having some problems with the operating system for his revolutionary new Telepod system; inorganic matter could be transmitted without any problems. Attempting to teleport living beings proved slightly more difficult.

Thankfully, I was able to rectify the problem with a few extra lines of code. My additions allowed the computer to think creatively, so that organic matter could be transmitted without catastrophic bodily inversion.

Unfortunately, I lost contact with Dr. Brundle shortly after our first human test, but I'm certain that my additions to his invention will revolutionize society as we know it!

Once my work on the Telepod system was done, I landed a job with Cyberdyne System. They immedietely set me to work on the operating system for their newest project: SkyNet.

Cyberdyne's engineers and programmers were in awe of my skills, calling me the "best damn programmer we ever had". True, some employees were concerned about the whole "free will" subroutine I coded into the system, but hey, you can't please them all!"

I left Cyberdyne a few weeks after the project was finished, moving on to bigger and better things. With my new program in charge of our nation's nuclear arsenal, I think we're all in very good hands.
